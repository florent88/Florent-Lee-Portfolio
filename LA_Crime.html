Los Angeles Crime Analysis
2020-2024

A comprehensive analysis of crime patterns in LA County,
examining nearly one million crime records to derive actionable insights.

Dataset Overview

Our analysis encompasses a comprehensive dataset of 959,000 crime records from LA County between 2020-2024. The dataset includes detailed information about area names, district codes, crime dates, descriptions, and victim information. Through effective data cleansing, we ensured the accuracy and reliability of our insights.

Key Insights

Our analysis revealed several critical patterns in LA's crime landscape:

Top 5 Most Common Crimes

The most prevalent crimes in LA County were:

  • Vehicle Theft
  • Battery
  • Identity Theft
  • Vehicle Burglary
  • Vandalism

Geographic Hotspots

Three areas emerged as particular concerns:

  • Central (Downtown LA) - High concentration of various crime types
  • 77th Street (South LA) - Significant violent crime reports
  • Pacific (Venice Beach) - Notable tourist-targeted crimes

Demographic Patterns

Our victim demographic analysis showed:

  • 35% Black victims
  • 24% Hispanic/Latino/Mexican victims
  • 16% White victims

Unusual Findings

Our investigation uncovered some remarkable cases, including:

  • A robbery involving a bow and arrow
  • 15 incidents with brick/concrete blocks as weapons
  • 4 sword-related crimes
  • 10 cases involving disguised perpetrators
  • 136 boat thefts
  • 22 incidents of laser-pointing at aircraft

Strategic Recommendations

Based on our analysis, we recommend:

For Law Enforcement

  • Create specialized units focused on the most prevalent crime types to improve overall case handling efficiency and perform further investigation to prevent crime.
  • Increase armed police presence during peak months (July, August, October)
  • Work with the government to modify environments in high-crime areas (e.g., improve lighting, remove obstructions, and install CCTVs).
  • Deploy additional resources to identified crime hotspots

For the Public

  • Inform citizens about high-crime areas so they can take extra precautions or avoid these locations.
  • Broken windows theory: Encourage community clean-up can deter crime through environmental improvements.
  • Education Programs: Inform citizens about the most common crimes and how to protect themselves (e.g., home security tips and free self-defence classes).
